Bad effects of smooking

Smoking has numerous harmful effects on the body and overall health. Here are the major bad effects: --- 1. Respiratory System Damage Chronic Bronchitis: Inflammation of the airways. Emphysema: Destruction of the lungs' air sacs. Increased risk of asthma attacks. Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD): A long-term, irreversible disease. --- 2. Cancer Risks Smoking is a major cause of various types of cancer: Lung cancer (most common) Mouth, throat, and esophagus cancer Stomach, pancreas, liver, bladder, and kidney cancers Cervical cancer in women Leukemia --- 3. Heart and Circulatory System Issues Increases blood pressure and heart rate. Causes atherosclerosis (narrowing of arteries). Leads to heart attack and stroke. Decreases blood flow to limbs, which may cause tissue death and amputation. --- 4. Reproductive and Sexual Health Problems Reduced fertility in both men and women. Erectile dysfunction in men. Pregnancy complications, such as miscarriage, premature birth, and birth defects. --- 5. Weakened Immune System Slower healing of wounds and infections. Higher risk of diseases like pneumonia and tuberculosis. --- 6. Effects on Appearance Yellowed teeth and bad breath. Premature aging and wrinkles. Hair loss and damaged skin. Smelly clothes and surroundings. --- 7. Effects on Others (Secondhand Smoke) Harms non-smokers, especially children and pregnant women. Causes asthma, SIDS, ear infections, and lung infections in children.
